Project Experience

National Undergraduate Projects of Innovation and Entrepreneurship:
Point Cloud Image-based Gesture Recognition and its Application on Human-Robot-Interaction System of "KeJia" Domestic Robot      May 2013 ∼ May 2014

This project is mainly focused on the abnormal behaviour detection and security guarantee for the old. The project is developed on ROS(Robot Operating System) and KeJia(The famous domestic robot in USTC). Two other partners cooperate with me on this project. The project is supervised by Pro. Xiaoping Chen and has been concluded in May,2014. We applied many valid algorithms, like SVM(Support Vector Machine), HOG(Histogram of Oriented Gradient), Adboost(Adaptive Boosting), PCA(Principal Component Analysis), etc. Our diligence guaranteed us to get a high appraisal in the final oral defense. We realized three main functions:

1. Gesture Recognition:
We realize the gesture recognition function. Robot can recognize different gestures, which can control the behaviors of the robot.
2. Fall-down Detection:
 The robot can detect the fall-down behaviors. After querrying the fall-down person, the robot will act according to the reaction of that person, like calling for help automatically or notifying the relatives.
3. Stranger Recognition:
 The robot can recognize the stranger, after comparing with the database and then it will react to the stranger by notifying the master or calling the police.

•   Features:

——  The robot can map the route and go on patrol automatically.
——  The master can control the robot's moving by gesture.
——  The robot can follow the master and detect his/her abnormal behaviour, like fall-down, and take the corresponding measures according to the master's response.
——  As the person comes into the house, the robot will follow the person and implement the face-recognition. After querying the local database, the robot can identify the stranger or the acquaintance.

Undergraduate Research Program:
 Fault Diagnosis in the Model Space        Dec. 2013 ∼ now

This project is mainly focused on the fault diagnosis in the model space. It is supervised by my mentor Pro. Huanhuan Chen and is scheduled to conclude till Oct. 2014.

Traditional approaches are mainly based on the prior knowledge of the known model system and are conducted in the signal space. However, under this situation, many problems may occur. For instance, many previous assumptions are needed, which may be not true in some situations; or the data needed for training in signal space are sometimes very limited, which may cause the model you train with these absent data in signal space peforms very badly in real situation; or some particular faults needed to train the model are very expensive, which raise the importance of cognitive fault diagnosis that you can train the model just from the normal data, without any faults involved, to name a few. The project is focused on a novel approach in mapping the temporal state(or signal space) to the model space and calculate the distance between different models, so that the fault diagnosis can be conducted in the model space. Here, the reservoir model is utilized to stand for the the data in the signal space. The reservoir model here is just like a deterministic ESN(Echo State Networks) and owns advantage of computational efficiency. This work partially has been conducted by my mentor Huanhuan Chen before, which you can see the latest paper here. Now I am trying to modify the algorithm using non-linear model and hope to get better performance.
